Frequently Asked Questions

What is CoderDojo?

CoderDojo is a free open global collaborative movement to teach youth to code. For more information, see our About page.

How can I get involved?

There are plenty of ways! We are always looking for mentors, sponsors and in-kind donations!

How does a session usually run?

Sessions are once a month on a weekend and generally run for 4 hours We begin with a demo and basic instructions (~15mins) Mentors go around the room and help youth with coding and their projects I am over the age of 18, can I still attend?

If you are an are not a parent, guardian or an observer from a partner organization, we ask that adult learners please consider other programs. CoderDojo is specifically for youth, there are many (free) Meetup groups and classes at General Assembly, Alley NYC, Brooklyn Brainery etc.

For Parents

Should I attend sessions with my child?

Parents of youth 13 or younger are required to be present during sessions for legal concerns.

How much does CoderDojo cost?

Nothing. Coder Dojo is 100% free as it is run by passionate volunteers. If you feel generous, you can always bring snacks for the kids :)

How should we prepare for sessions?

We ask that please bring a laptop. (We are working on getting sponsorships to purchase loaner laptops.) Topics that we will be discussing are listed on the registration page of each session. Prior knowledge is not necessary unless specified.

For Mentors

What is required to become a mentor?

Basic programming knowledge and computer skills Attend one of our volunteer sessions A love of learning!

How should I prepare for sessions?

We ask that you please review the materials and languages that we will be covering at the upcoming session. You are welcome to bring your own laptop, but most time will be spend going around helping youth.
